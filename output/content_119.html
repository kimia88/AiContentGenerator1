Introduction

Fashion is not merely about protection and adornment; it is a powerful medium through which individuals express their identity, values, and beliefs. It serves as a visual language that communicates one's personality, culture, and social status. Throughout history, fashion has been a reflection of societal changes, technological advancements, and artistic movements. It has the ability to evoke emotions, challenge norms, and inspire creativity. In today's globalized world, fashion plays a significant role in shaping cultural identities and fostering cross-cultural understanding. It is a dynamic industry that continually evolves, influencing not only what people wear but also how they perceive themselves and others.
