Introduction

Nurses are healthcare professionals who play a vital role in patient care and recovery. They are often the backbone of the healthcare system, providing essential support and care to patients in various settings, including hospitals, clinics, and long-term care facilities. Nurses are responsible for administering medications, monitoring patients' vital signs, assisting with treatments, and educating patients and their families about managing health conditions. They collaborate with doctors and other healthcare professionals to ensure comprehensive care and are often the primary point of contact for patients and their families. Nurses develop strong relationships with patients, offering not only medical care but also emotional support, which can significantly impact patient outcomes. With their dedication and expertise, nurses are indispensable in promoting patient well-being and recovery.
