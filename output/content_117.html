Introduction

Nursing is often referred to as the heart of healthcare, playing a vital role in patient care, advocacy, and support. Nurses are the backbone of the medical profession, providing compassionate care and making a significant impact on patients' lives. They are responsible for assessing patients' needs, administering medications, performing diagnostic tests, and collaborating with other healthcare professionals to develop treatment plans. Nurses also play a crucial role in educating patients and their families about managing illnesses and promoting overall well-being. With their dedication, expertise, and empathy, nurses are instrumental in improving health outcomes and enhancing the quality of care in various healthcare settings.
